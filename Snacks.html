Chaat Mix

A healthy, spicy Indian-style oats dish made with veggies, perfect for quick breakfasts and full of fiber.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled potatoes
  • 2 tbsp tamarind chutney
  • 2 tbsp green chutney
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Sev and coriander leaves for garnish

Preparation Steps

  1. Mix chickpeas, onions, tomatoes, and potatoes in a bowl.
  2. Add chutneys, chaat masala, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Garnish with sev and chopped coriander before serving.

Fluffy Pav Bhaji

These fluffy pav-bhaji are soft, golden, and perfect with syrup, butter, or berries — a breakfast classic loved worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potatoes, cauliflower, peas, carrots)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 capsicum (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon wedges and coriander leaves for garnish
  • 4 pav buns

Preparation Steps

  1. Boil and mash all vegetables.
  2. Sauté onions, capsicum, and tomatoes in butter until soft.
  3. Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, and salt.
  4. Mix in mashed vegetables and simmer. Add water to adjust consistency.
  5. Toast pav buns with butter and serve with bhaji garnished with lemon and coriander.

Classic Kathi Roll

A delightful breakfast made by soaking bread in an egg mixture and frying to golden perfection. Best served with maple syrup or fruits.

Ingredients

  • 2 parathas or rotis
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or paneer
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup capsicum (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Coriander chutney and mayonnaise (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat oil in pan. Add onions and capsicum. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add chicken/paneer, spices, and salt. Cook for 5-6 minutes.
  3. Warm parathas, spread chutney and mayo (optional).
  4. Place filling, roll up tightly, and serve hot.

Spicy Paneer Roll

Traditional Samosa Recipe

Samosa is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake that's healthy, delicious, and perfect for breakfast or as a snack. These soft and fluffy cakes are naturally gluten-free and vegan.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups samosa rice (or regular parboiled rice)
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil or ghee (for greasing the samosa plates)

Preparation Steps

  1. Soaking: Wash rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak rice and fenugreek together, and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding: Grind urad dal first to a smooth, fluffy consistency. Then grind rice to a slightly coarse paste. Mix both batters together with salt.
  3. Fermentation: Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter will almost double in volume.
  4. Steaming: Grease samosa plates, pour batter into each mold, and steam for 10-12 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  5. Serving: Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or your favorite accompaniment.

Aloo Tikki Recipe

Aloo Tikki is a popular North Indian breakfast of flatbread stuffed with a spiced potato filling, served hot with butter, curd, or pickle.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt, coriander, oil/ghee

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare dough with flour, water, and salt. Let it rest.
  2. Mix mashed potatoes with spices and herbs.
  3. Roll dough balls, fill with stuffing, seal and roll again.
  4. Cook on tawa with ghee until golden on both sides.

Crispy Kachori Recipe

Kachori is a thin, crispy South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It's a versatile dish that can be served plain or stuffed with various fillings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice (regular or parboiled)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Preparation Steps

  1. Soaking: Soak rice and dal separately with fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding: Grind dal first to smooth consistency, then grind rice coarsely. Mix together with salt.
  3. Fermentation: Let batter ferment overnight (8-12 hours) until bubbly and doubled in volume.
  4. Cooking: Heat a griddle, pour ladle of batter, spread in circular motion to make thin crepe.
  5. Frying: Drizzle oil/ghee, cook until golden and crisp. Fold and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Crispy Medu Pakora Recipe

Medu Pakora is a popular South Indian savory donut made from urad dal. These crispy, fluffy fritters are perfect for breakfast or as a snack with coconut chutney and sambar.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 inch ginger (grated)
  • 10-12 curry leaves (chopped)
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Preparation Steps

  1. Soaking: Soak urad dal for 4-5 hours. Drain completely.
  2. Grinding: Grind to smooth, fluffy batter with minimal water.
  3. Mixing: Add chilies, ginger, curry leaves, hing, cumin and salt. Whip well.
  4. Shaping: Wet hands, take batter, make hole in center to form donut shape.
  5. Frying: Deep fry in medium-hot oil until golden brown and crisp.
  6. Serving: Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Fluffy Dhokla Recipe

Dhokla is a deep-fried Indian bread made from whole wheat flour. These golden, puffy breads are perfect for breakfast or special occasions, often served with potato curry or chana masala.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 tablespoon semolina (sooji/rava) - optional
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Water (as needed to make dough)
  • Oil for deep frying

Preparation Steps

  1. Dough: Mix flour, semolina, salt and 1 tbsp oil. Add water gradually to make stiff dough.
  2. Kneading: Knead well for 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Shaping: Divide dough into small balls. Roll each into 3-4 inch circles.
  4. Frying: Heat oil, gently slide dhokla in. Press with slotted spoon to puff up.
  5. Draining: Flip once, fry till golden. Drain on paper towels.
  6. Serving: Serve hot with potato curry, chana masala or pickle.

Sev Puri Recipe

A comforting South Indian rice-lentil porridge tempered with ghee, cumin, and black pepper, traditionally served during Makara Sankranti.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida
  • 10-12 cashews (optional)
  • 1 inch ginger (finely chopped)
  • 10 curry leaves
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps

  1. Dry roast moong dal until fragrant. Wash with rice and drain.
  2. Pressure cook with water for 5-6 whistles until mushy.
  3. Mash the mixture slightly. Add salt and more water if needed.
  4. Heat ghee in pan. Temper with cumin, pepper, cashews, ginger, curry leaves and asafoetida.
  5. Pour tempering over sev-puri. Mix well and serve hot with sambar or coconut chutney.

Bhel Puri Recipe

Bhel Puri is a classic South Indian breakfast dish made with semolina (rava), seasoned with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and vegetables. This wholesome dish is quick to prepare and packed with flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fine semolina (rava/sooji)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 tsp chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 10-12 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans - optional)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps

  1. Dry roast the semolina: In a pan, dry roast the semolina on medium heat for 4-5 minutes until it turns aromatic and light golden. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  2. Prepare the tempering: Heat ghee/oil in the same pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add urad dal and chana dal, sauté until golden.
  3. Add aromatics: Add green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Cook vegetables: Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent. If using vegetables, add them now and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Boil water: Add 2 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt. Bring to a rolling boil.
  6. Add semolina: Reduce heat to low. Slowly add the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  7. Cook covered: Cover and cook on low heat for 3-4 minutes until the water is absorbed and bhel-puri is fluffy.
  8. Finish with garnishes: Turn off heat. Add lemon juice and chopped coriander. Mix well.
  9. Serve hot: Fluff with a fork and serve immediately with coconut chutney or pickle.

Chef's Tips

  • For richer flavor, use ghee instead of oil
  • Roasting semolina well is key to prevent stickiness
  • Add water carefully - too much makes it mushy, too little makes it dry
  • For variation, add cashews or peanuts during tempering

Serving suggestion: Pair with coconut chutney and filter coffee for authentic South Indian breakfast experience.
