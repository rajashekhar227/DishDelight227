Kheer (Rice Pudding)

A creamy Indian dessert made with rice, milk, and sugar, flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, pistachios)
  • 1 tbsp raisins (optional)
  • Saffron strands (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse rice well and soak for 30 minutes. Drain before using.
  2. Boil milk in a heavy-bottomed pan, then reduce heat and add the soaked rice.
  3. Simmer on low, stirring often, until rice is cooked and milk thickens (about 40 mins).
  4. Add sugar, cardamom, saffron, and nuts. Stir well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with more nuts.

Fudgy Chocolate Brownies

Rich and gooey chocolate brownies with a crackly top and soft center – a timeless dessert treat!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • Optional: chocolate chips or chopped nuts

Preparation Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease a square baking pan.
  2. Mix melted butter, sugar, eggs, and vanilla in a bowl.
  3. Sift in cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder. Mix gently.
  4. Fold in chocolate chips or nuts if using.
  5. Pour batter into the pan and spread evenly.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes or until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs.
  7. Cool before cutting into squares. Enjoy warm or chilled.

Fruit Custard

A light and refreshing dessert made with creamy custard and seasonal fruits – great for summer!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tbsp custard powder (vanilla flavor)
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped apples
  • 1/2 cup chopped bananas
  • 1/2 cup grapes
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • Chopped nuts (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Mix custard powder in 1/4 cup cold milk to form a smooth paste.
  2. Boil the rest of the milk with sugar. Reduce heat and add the custard paste while stirring.
  3. Cook for 3-5 minutes until thick. Let it cool, then refrigerate for 1 hour.
  4. Mix in the chopped fruits just before serving. Top with nuts if desired.

Carrot Halwa (Gajar ka Halwa)

A rich North Indian dessert made with grated carrots, milk, sugar, and ghee, slow-cooked to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups grated carrots (about 500g)
  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 10 cashews and 10 almonds (chopped)
  • 5-6 raisins

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a pan and roast cashews, almonds, and raisins until golden. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add grated carrots and sauté for 5–7 minutes until slightly soft.
  3. Pour in the milk and cook on medium flame. Stir occasionally.
  4. Let it simmer until milk reduces and thickens. Stir to avoid burning.
  5. Add sugar and cook until it melts and blends in. The mixture will turn slightly glossy.
  6. Mix in the remaining ghee and cardamom powder. Cook for a few more minutes until the halwa thickens.
  7. Finally, add roasted dry fruits and mix well.
  8. Serve warm or chilled as per preference.

Gulab Jamun

A classic Indian dessert made of deep-fried milk dumplings soaked in aromatic sugar syrup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Milk (as needed to form dough)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • For syrup: 1.5 cups sugar, 1.5 cups water, 3 cardamom pods, few drops of rose water

Preparation Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, flour, baking soda and ghee.
  2. Add milk little by little to form a soft, smooth dough. Let it rest for 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the syrup by boiling sugar, water, and cardamom until slightly sticky. Add rose water and keep warm.
  4. Divide the dough into small smooth balls with no cracks.
  5. Heat oil/ghee on low-medium heat and fry the balls until golden brown.
  6. Soak the fried balls in warm syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Rasgulla

Soft and spongy white cheese balls cooked in light sugar syrup — a traditional Bengali delight.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar (to curdle milk)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods

Preparation Steps

  1. Boil the milk. Once it starts boiling, add lemon juice and stir gently until it curdles completely.
  2. Strain using a muslin cloth. Wash the chenna (curdled milk) under cold water and squeeze excess water.
  3. Hang the cloth for 30 minutes to drain all moisture.
  4. Knead the chenna for 10–15 minutes until smooth and no lumps remain.
  5. Make small smooth balls with no cracks.
  6. Boil sugar and water with cardamom in a wide pot to make the syrup.
  7. Gently drop the balls into the boiling syrup. Cover and cook for 15–20 minutes, until they double in size.
  8. Let them cool in syrup before serving. Chill if desired.
